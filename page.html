Recycle Park April Tags

For my final project, I continued my work on the April Tags used for the Recycle Park project.

The physical model being setup at the final presentation to the community. The two cameras on bottom are used to track the tags on the bottom of the physcial models through an acrylic window on the bottom of the park.

Here you can see the tracking of the April Tags through the acrylic window. As you can see the tags are attached to the bottom of the individual models. This is the view of one of the cameras.

Travis Chambers

The Recycle Park project was a collaboration between the Reality Computing students and the Architecture students in UDBS. Recycle Park is a park in Pittsburgh that is being renovated with the help of UDBS. Our role in this process was to use virtual reality to help the community take an active role in the renovation by allowing them to help plan the new park.

Pictured here is a sample of the April Tags used. Beneath each group of four is a family name and for our project we used the 36h11 family.

Although these are different sizes, this shows the difference between the stock April Tags on the right and final tags we used on the right. As you can see the id of the tag was moved and made smaller and a lot of white space was removed also in order to make the tags as small as possible. The size of the tags was important because they had to be big enough to be seen by the cameras and also small enough that they would fit on the bottom of the models.
